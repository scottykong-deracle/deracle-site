Frequently Asked Questions

+ What is eMMC?

eMMC integrates NAND Flash memory and a flash controller into a single JEDEC-standard package, providing a compact, cost-effective and reliable embedded storage solution for industrial and embedded systems.

+ What is the difference between eMMC and UFS?

eMMC is widely used for cost-effective embedded storage, while UFS utilizes a full-duplex serial interface to deliver significantly higher performance, lower latency and improved multitasking capability.

+ When should SPI NAND Flash be used?

SPI NAND Flash is commonly selected for embedded Linux systems, IoT devices and industrial controllers requiring compact boot storage, simple interfaces and low power consumption.

+ What is LPDDR memory?

LPDDR is a low-power DRAM technology optimized for embedded computing, AI edge devices and battery-powered systems where high bandwidth and low energy consumption are essential.
