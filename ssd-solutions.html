Frequently Asked Questions

+ What is the difference between SATA and NVMe SSD?

SATA SSD uses the SATA interface with speeds up to approximately 550 MB/s, while NVMe SSD uses the PCIe interface to deliver significantly higher performance, lower latency and better scalability for demanding applications.

+ Which SSD is better for industrial applications?

Both SATA and NVMe SSDs are suitable for industrial systems. SATA SSDs are widely used for compatibility and cost efficiency, while NVMe SSDs are preferred for applications requiring high-speed data transfer and intensive workloads.

+ What is the difference between M.2 2242 and M.2 2280?

The numbers indicate the physical dimensions of the module. M.2 2242 measures 22 × 42 mm, while M.2 2280 measures 22 × 80 mm. The appropriate form factor depends on the available installation space and system design.

+ Can SSD replace HDD?

Yes. In most applications, SSDs can directly replace HDDs while providing faster boot times, lower power consumption, higher reliability and improved overall system performance.
